Semester 1 Course

Fundamentals of Computers and Office Productivity Tools

A beginner-friendly course for understanding computers and building practical confidence with common office tools.

Course Introduction

This course introduces students to computer fundamentals, hardware, software, operating systems, basic programming concepts, and common office productivity tools used in academic and professional work.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand computer basics in simple language.
  • Identify hardware, software, memory, and storage devices.
  • Use office tools for documents, spreadsheets, and presentations.
  • Develop practical confidence in the computer lab.

Teaching Approach

Topics will be explained slowly with diagrams, daily-life examples, bilingual support where needed, classroom discussion, and repeated lab practice.

Assessment Components

  • Attendance and class participation
  • Assignments and quizzes
  • Practical lab performance
  • Internal tests and final examination

Practical Importance

Lab work is essential. Students should practice file management, typing, document formatting, spreadsheet formulas, presentations, and safe internet use.

Suggested Study Method

Read the lecture notes before class, revise after class, complete assignments before the due date, attempt quizzes for revision, and repeat lab activities until comfortable.
